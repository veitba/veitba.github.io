The Project


Within our master design studio at the University of Innsbruck we started with the general topic of Los Angeles, it’s current topics of social, cultural movements and took a closer look into different neighborhoods and their qualities.

Throughout our research we were really interested in the park situation in Los Angeles and made some statistics about the reachable park areas in comparison to the density of inhabitants. The result was, that Compton turned out to have the least green space per person, even though the demographics show, that Compton hosts many families with small children.

As we found out about this fact, we started to search for possibilities to increase the size of public and green space.

By looking closer into the urban structures of Compton, we found a surprisingly and outstanding looking neighborhood in between the W Raymond St and the W Greenleaf Blvd. The properties in this area differ from the average properties in Compton. The parcels usually have an average sized building at the front of the street, a garage and a very long backyard. As far as we could tell from our analysis with satellite pictures, most parts of the backyards are being barely used. We identified this situation as a chance to develop a whole new idea of open space for this neighborhood.

Our idea for this neighborhood is based on the concept of sharing space and being conscious about the usage of the backyards. In our project we would like to suggest the following steps to reach a new era of neighborhood cohabitation: Transform the trash areas into wildflower beds, plant a tree for each car and boat in the backyards, remove the walls in between the properties to create wide open space.

This project of us is supposed to be a thought-provoking impulse, to show what hidden qualities are being captured in your own backyards.

We are curious about your thoughts and feelings of how we are dealing with the situation in your neighborhood. Feel free to share your opinion with us!
